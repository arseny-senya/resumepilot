Как написать сопроводительное письмо в 2026 году

Сопроводительное письмо помогает работодателю понять, почему именно вы заинтересованы в вакансии и чем можете быть полезны компании. Хорошо написанное письмо не заменяет резюме, но значительно увеличивает шансы получить приглашение на собеседование.

What Is a Cover Letter?

A cover letter is a short message sent together with your resume. Its purpose is to explain why you're interested in the position and why you're a good fit for the company.

Unlike a resume, a cover letter gives you the opportunity to communicate directly with the employer and show genuine interest in the role.

Do You Always Need a Cover Letter?

Not always. Some employers don't require one, while others specifically ask applicants to include it.

However, submitting a well-written cover letter can help you stand out, especially when applying for competitive positions.

When a Cover Letter Is Especially Helpful

  • You're changing careers.
  • You have little or no work experience.
  • You're applying to an international company.
  • You want to explain gaps in your employment history.
  • You want to demonstrate genuine interest in the company.

Simple Cover Letter Structure

A professional cover letter usually follows a simple structure:

  1. Greeting.
  2. Introduce yourself.
  3. Explain why you're interested in the position.
  4. Describe why you're a strong candidate.
  5. Thank the employer.
  6. Sign your name.

Example of a Weak Cover Letter

❌ Poor Example

Hello.

I'm looking for a job.
Please hire me.

Thank you.

Example of a Strong Cover Letter

✅ Good Example

Hello!

I was excited to see your Frontend Developer position because I've been building modern web applications using JavaScript and React for the past two years.

I enjoy creating fast, user-friendly interfaces and would love the opportunity to contribute to your team.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to discussing how my skills can benefit your company.

Best regards,
John Smith

What You Should Avoid

  • Using the same letter for every company.
  • Saying "I desperately need this job."
  • Repeating your entire resume.
  • Making the letter too long.
  • Writing generic phrases without specifics.

Cover Letter Tips

  • Keep it between 150 and 250 words.
  • Address the hiring manager by name if possible.
  • Show why you're interested in this specific company.
  • Focus on what value you can bring.
  • Finish with a confident call to action.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a cover letter be?

Around 150–250 words. Keep it concise while including the most important information.

Can I use the same cover letter for every application?

It's better to customize your letter for each employer. Even small changes show genuine interest.

Should I repeat my resume?

No. Your cover letter should complement your resume, not duplicate it.

Do I need a cover letter if it's optional?

If you have the opportunity to include one, it's often worth the effort because it can help you stand out from other applicants.
