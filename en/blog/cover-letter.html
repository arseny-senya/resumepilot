How to Write a Cover Letter in 2026

A cover letter helps employers understand why you're interested in their company and why you're a strong candidate for the position. While it doesn't replace your resume, a well-written cover letter can significantly increase your chances of getting an interview.

What Is a Cover Letter?

A cover letter is a short document or email sent together with your resume. It introduces you to the employer, explains your interest in the position, and highlights why you're a good fit.

Unlike a resume, a cover letter gives you the opportunity to speak directly to the hiring manager and show genuine enthusiasm for the company.

Do You Always Need a Cover Letter?

Not always. Some employers don't ask for one, while others make it a required part of the application.

Even when it's optional, including a thoughtful cover letter can help you stand out from other applicants.

When a Cover Letter Is Most Helpful

  • You're changing careers.
  • You have little or no professional experience.
  • You're applying for an international position.
  • You want to explain employment gaps.
  • You want to demonstrate genuine interest in the company.

Simple Cover Letter Structure

A professional cover letter usually follows this structure:

  1. Greeting.
  2. Introduce yourself.
  3. Explain why you're interested in the position.
  4. Describe why you're a strong candidate.
  5. Thank the employer.
  6. Sign your name.

Example of a Weak Cover Letter

❌ Poor Example

Hello.

I'm looking for a job.
Please hire me.

Thank you.

Example of a Strong Cover Letter

✅ Good Example

Dear Hiring Manager,

I was excited to see your Frontend Developer opening because I enjoy building modern, user-friendly web applications with JavaScript and React.

Over the past two years I've worked on responsive websites and web applications while continuously improving my skills. I'm confident my experience and enthusiasm would allow me to contribute to your team.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications in more detail.

Kind regards,
John Smith

What You Should Never Write

A cover letter should sound confident and professional. Avoid phrases that make you appear desperate or unprepared.

  • Don't write "I really need this job."
  • Don't claim you're the perfect candidate without evidence.
  • Don't copy the same letter for every application.
  • Don't repeat your resume word for word.
  • Don't make the letter longer than one page.

Common Cover Letter Mistakes

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Using a generic template without customization.
  • Writing long paragraphs.
  • Talking only about what you want.
  • Ignoring the company's requirements.
  • Grammar and spelling mistakes.
  • Repeating information already found in your resume.

Tips for Writing a Better Cover Letter

✅ Best Practices

  • Keep it between 150 and 250 words.
  • Personalize each letter for the company.
  • Focus on how you can help the employer.
  • Use clear and professional language.
  • End with confidence and gratitude.

Cover Letter Checklist

Before sending your application, make sure your cover letter:

  • Introduces you clearly.
  • Explains why you're interested in the role.
  • Highlights relevant skills and achievements.
  • Matches the job description.
  • Contains no spelling or grammar mistakes.
  • Ends with a professional closing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a cover letter be?

Around 150–250 words is ideal. Recruiters usually spend less than a minute reading it, so keep it concise.

Should every job application include a cover letter?

If the employer allows it, including a cover letter is usually a good idea because it helps you stand out from other applicants.

Can I use AI to write a cover letter?

Yes, but always personalize the final version. Recruiters can often recognize generic AI-generated text.

Should I send a cover letter as a PDF?

If the employer asks for attachments, PDF is usually the safest choice because it preserves formatting. If there's a message box in the application form, paste your cover letter there instead.
